About Airwareness

Air quality has a major effect on human health and contributes to a growing list of short and long-term health conditions. There are no safe levels of exposure to harmful air pollutants.

Australia’s transport emissions have risen faster than any sector, causing 70% of pollution levels in the urban environment.

A growing population, combined with high-density urban living and an increased use of diesel vehicles has intensified health issues caused by pollution.

Australia’s air pollution causes a mortality rate double the national road toll. To unearth this problem, we need to be clear with what is unseen.



Airwareness connects the health of people to their immediate environment. Driven by science and technology, we turn environmental data into clear and compelling information.

By making the invisible visible, we assess key locations, identify current problems, and provide useful information and best practice solutions.
We track outcomes to ensure a healthier future for our communities.

Airwareness brings clarity around air quality, presenting a message understood by all.
